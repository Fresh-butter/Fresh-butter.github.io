Motihari

I was born and raised in Motihari, East Champaran, Bihar— a town rich in history, culture, and scenic charm. It's renowned for its association with Mahatma Gandhi’s Champaran Satyagraha, and its peaceful environment continues to inspire generations.

The historic Kesariya Stupa, one of the tallest and largest Buddhist stupas in the world.

The sacred Someshwarnath Temple, a revered Shiva temple in Areraj.

The birthplace of George Orwell, author of *1984* and *Animal Farm*, located in Motihari.

The Ashokan Pillar at Lauria Areraj—an enduring symbol of ancient Mauryan legacy.

Gandhi Sangrahalaya, a museum dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi’s historic Champaran Satyagraha.

Growing up in Motihari instilled in me a deep appreciation for simplicity, resilience, and history. The town’s legacy continues to shape my values and worldview. From the vibrant markets to the tranquil countryside, every corner of Motihari holds a memory. Its rich blend of tradition and progress has deeply influenced my identity.
